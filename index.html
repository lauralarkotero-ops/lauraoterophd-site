AI is redefining what it means to be an instructional designer. As a researcher and professor, I study that shift and prepare the ID leaders who will navigate it.

Currently available for consulting engagements in AI integration, instructional design leadership, and change management.

My research looks at the professional identity and evolving role of instructional designers in higher education: specifically, how generative AI is reshaping our ability to collaborate with faculty, build social capital, and lead change inside academic institutions. As AI absorbs more of the technical work of course design, I study what that means for human expertise, professional credibility, and the future of the field.

That research is grounded in my own experience leading change on the ground, as an instructional designer and educational technologist at Hartnell College, CSU Monterey Bay (where I served as Online Education Coordinator and adjunct faculty), and the Naval Postgraduate School's NPS Extended Campus, and now as an Assistant Professor at San José State University, where I also serve as Program Coordinator for the Master of Science in Instructional Design and Technology (MSIDT) program. See this work in practice in my portfolio, or view my full CV.

I hold a Ph.D. in Leadership with a specialization in Instructional Technology from the University of the Cumberlands, a Master's in Education from CSU East Bay, and a Bachelor's in Communication from CSU Monterey Bay, after transferring from a California Community College.

I'm based in Monterey, California, with my husband, daughter, and our menagerie of creatures and plants. I'm also a creative writer. You can find that work at elleotero.com.

What I Do

The Future of the ID Profession

I study how generative AI is redefining the role of instructional designers in academia: our professional identity, our credibility, and our ability to build the collaborative relationships that make good course design possible. I continue to publish in this area.

Leading Change

At our core, instructional designers are change leaders. We build relationships, empower clients to grow their skills, and develop the training and support programs that make change stick. Check out my portfolio, or reach out to see how I can help your organization.

Instructional Design & Technology

I've worked as an instructional designer and technologist for over fifteen years. Technology always changes, but the principles behind effective training don't. That's why a seasoned designer can help any team adopt new tools, from VR/XR to generative AI.

Today, I am available for consultations related to generative AI integration, instructional design leadership, change management, and faculty/staff development programs.
