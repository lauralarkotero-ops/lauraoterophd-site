My name is Laura Otero. I am an educator, instructional designer, and researcher in the field of instructional technology and higher ed.

I am currently available for consulting engagements.

I'm based in Monterey, California, with my husband, daughter, and our menagerie of creatures and plants. Read on to learn more about my work, or explore my professional portfolio.

I'm also a creative writer — you can find my creative work at elleotero.com.

I started at a California Community College, transferred to earn my Bachelor's in Communication at CSU Monterey Bay, then completed a Master's in Education at CSU East Bay and a Ph.D. in Leadership with a specialization in Instructional Technology at the University of the Cumberlands.

My career has taken me through instructional design and educational technology roles at Hartnell College, CSU Monterey Bay — where I served as Online Education Coordinator and adjunct faculty — and the Naval Postgraduate School, where I coordinated instructional design for the Department of Defense's NPS Extended Campus. Today, I'm an Assistant Professor at San José State University, where I also serve as Program Coordinator for the Master of Science in Instructional Design and Technology (MSIDT) program.

My research explores how generative AI is reshaping the social capital and collaborative relationships between faculty and instructional designers in higher education. Drawing on organizational psychology and my own experience as a practicing instructional designer, I study how technology mediates workplace relationships, professional development, and the future of human expertise in AI-enhanced educational environments.

What I Do

Technology Leadership

Leadership shapes collaboration. My research examines how relational leadership behaviors affect the working relationship between instructional designers and faculty in higher education, and I continue to publish in this area.

Instructional Design & Technology

I've worked as an instructional designer and technologist for over fifteen years. Technology always changes, but the principles behind effective training don't — which is why a seasoned designer can help any team adopt new tools, from VR/XR to generative AI.

Managing Change

At our core, instructional designers are change leaders — building relationships, empowering clients to grow their skills, and developing the training and support programs that make change stick. Check out my portfolio, or reach out to see how I can help your organization.

Today, I am available for consultations related to leadership, change management, instructional technology, generative AI integration, and faculty/staff development programs.
